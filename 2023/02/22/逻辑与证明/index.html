    Proposition and Compound Proposition

    Proposition 非真即假的陈述句
    Compound Proposition 已知命题用逻辑运算符组合而成
    Truth table 列出命题的所有可能真值

    Logical connectives

    Let p and q are propositions.
    ==Negation(not)==&emsp; $\neg$p
    ==Conjunction(and)==&emsp; p$\wedge$q
    ==Disjunction(or)==&emsp; p$\vee$q

    ==Exclusive Or(xor)==&emsp;p$\oplus$q

    p q p$\oplus$q
    T T F
    T F T
    F T T
    F F F

    ==Implication(Conditional Statement)==&emsp;p$\rightarrow$q
    “if p, then q”
    p is the hypothesis or premise
    q is the conclusion

    p q p$\rightarrow$q
    T T T
    T F F
    F T T
    F F T

    Converse of p$\rightarrow$q is q $\rightarrow$ p
    Contrapositive of p$\rightarrow$q is $\neg$q $\rightarrow$ $\neg$p
    Inverse of p$\rightarrow$q is $\neg$p $\rightarrow$ $\neg$q

    ==Biconditional Statement(bi-implication)==&emsp;p$\leftrightarrow$q
    “p if and only if q” or “p iff q”

    p q p$\leftrightarrow$q
    T T T
    T F F
    F T F
    F F T

    Order of precedence

    If same : from left to right
    $\neg$ > $\wedge$ > $\vee$ > $\rightarrow$ > $\leftrightarrow$
    eg:
    p $\rightarrow$ q $\rightarrow$ r $\vee$ $\neg$s $\wedge$ t
    (p $\rightarrow$ q) $\rightarrow$ (r $\vee$ ($\neg$s $\wedge$ t))

    Logic Equivalence

    Tautology : a compound proposition that is always true for all possible truth values
    Contradiction : a compound proposition that is always false for all possible truth values
    Contingency : a compound proposition that is neither a tautology nor a contradiction

    Equivalent Propositions

    The propositions p and q are called logically equivalent if p $\leftrightarrow$ q is a tautology
    denoted by p $\equiv$ q or p $\Leftrightarrow$ q

    Important Logical Equivalences

    • Identity laws
      • $p \wedge T \equiv p$
      • $p \vee F \equiv p$
    • Domination laws
      • $p \vee T \equiv T$
      • $p \wedge F \equiv F$
    • Idempotent laws
      • $p \vee p \equiv p$
      • $p \wedge p \equiv p$
    • Double negation law
      • $\neg (\neg p) \equiv p$
    • Commutative laws
      • $p \vee q \equiv q \vee p$
      • $p \wedge q \equiv q \wedge p$
    • Associative laws
      • $(p \vee q) \vee r \equiv p \vee (q \vee r)$
      • $(p \wedge q) \wedge r \equiv p \wedge (q \wedge r)$
    • Distributive laws
      • $p \wedge (q \vee r) \equiv (p \wedge q) \vee (p \wedge r)$
      • $p \vee (q \wedge r) \equiv (p \vee q) \wedge (p \vee r)$
    • De Morgan’s laws
      • $\neg (p \vee q) \equiv \neg p \wedge \neg q$
      • $\neg (p \wedge q) \equiv \neg p \vee \neg q$
    • Absorption laws
      • $p \vee (p \wedge q) \equiv p$
      • $p \wedge (p \vee q) \equiv p$
    • Negation laws
      • $p \vee \neg p \equiv T$
      • $p \wedge \neg p \equiv F$
    • Useful law
      • $p \rightarrow q \equiv \neg p \vee q$

    By definition
    $p \oplus q \equiv (p \wedge \neg q) \vee (\neg p \wedge q)$
    $p \leftrightarrow q \equiv (p \rightarrow q) \wedge (q \rightarrow p)$

    Predicate Logic

    Constant : specific object
    Variable : object in universe(所有可以代入变量的值集合)
    Predicate : properties or relations among objects
    truth set : 使命题为真的值集合(subset of universe)
    P(x) is not a proposition, but after the substitution(all varibles) it becomes one.

    Assume Prime(x) where the universe of discourse is integers
    Prime(2)&emsp;T
    Prime(6)&emsp;F

    Quantified Statements

    Universally quantified : for every x, P(x) is true
    $\forall x\ P(x)$
    Existentially quantified
    $\exists x\ P(x)$
    The universe matters.

    The quantifiers have higher precedence than other logical operators.
    $\forall / \exists$ > $\neg$ > $\wedge$ > $\vee$ > $\rightarrow$ > $\leftrightarrow$
    eg:
    $\exists x \ P(x) \vee Q(x)$
    $(\exists x \ P(x)) \vee Q(x)$

    Translation with Quantifiers

    All SUSTech students are smart.
    use $\rightarrow$ not $\wedge$
    $\forall x \ (At(x, SUSTech)\rightarrow Smart(x))$ $universe : all \ students$
    $\forall x \ (Student(x)\wedge At(x, SUSTech)\rightarrow Smart(x))$ $universe : all \ people$

    Someone at SUSTech is smart.
    use $\wedge$ not $\rightarrow$
    $\exists x \ (At(x, SUSTech)\wedge Smart(x))$ $universe : all \ people$

    Negation of Quantified Statements

    De Morgan’s laws for quantifiers

    Negation Equivalent Statement
    $\neg \ \exists x\ P(x)$ $\forall x\ \neg P(x)$
    $\neg \ \forall x\ P(x)$ $\exists x\ \neg P(x)$
    $\neg \forall x \exists y \ P(x,y)$ $\exists x \forall y \ \neg P(x,y)$
    $\neg \exists x \forall y \ P(x,y)$ $\forall x \exists y \ \neg P(x,y)$

    Order of Quentifiers

    eg : L(x, y) denotes “x loves y”
    $\forall x \exists y \ L(x,y)$ : Everybody loves somebody.
    $\exists y \forall x \ L(x,y)$ : There is someone who is loved by everyone.
    $\exists y \forall x \ \neg L(x,y)$ : There is somebody whom no one loves.

    There is exactly one person whom everybody loves.
    $\exists x(\forall y L(y,x) \wedge \forall z (\forall w L(w,z) \rightarrow z = x))$
    There are exactly two people whom Lynn loves.
    $\exists x \exists y ((x \neq y \wedge L(Lynn,x) \wedge L(Lynn,y) \wedge (\forall z(L(Lynn,z)\rightarrow (z = x \vee z = y))))$

    Rules of Inference for Propositional Logic

    • Modus ponens
      $p \rightarrow q$
      $p$
      $\therefore q$
    • Modus tollens
      $p \rightarrow q$
      $\neg q$
      $\therefore \neg p$
    • Hypothetical syllogism
      $p \rightarrow q$
      $q \rightarrow r$
      $\therefore p \rightarrow r$
    • Disjunctive syllogism
      $p \vee q$
      $\neg p$
      $\therefore q$
    • Addition
      $p$
      $\therefore p \vee q$
    • Simplification
      $p \wedge q$
      $\therefore q$
    • Conjunction
      $p$
      $q$
      $\therefore p \wedge q$
    • Resolution
      $\neg p \vee r$
      $p \vee q$
      $\therefore q \wedge r$

    Rules of Inference for Quantified Statements

    • Universal Instantiation (UI)
      $\forall xP(x)$
      $\therefore P(c)$
    • Universal Generalization (UG)
      $P(c) \ for \ an \ arbitrary \ c$
      $\therefore \forall x P(x)$
    • Existential Instantiation (EI)
      $\exists xP(x)$
      $\therefore P(c) \ for\ some\ element\ c$
    • Existential Generalization (EG)
      $P(c) \ for \ some \ element \ c$
      $\therefore \exists x P(x)$
